Cream Cheese Wontons

Description

Also commonly called crab rangoon when imitation crab is added to filling. Essentially crispy, fried dumplings filled with cream cheese and onion normally found in American Chinese takeouts. Makes about 3 dozen wontons.

Ingredients

    For the Wontons:

  • 8 oz cream cheese
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 4 scallions
  • 1 pack wanton wrappers
  • vegetable oil for frying

    • For the Dipping Sauce:

  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon siracha (optional)

Directions

  1. Make the wonton filling by combining the cream cheese, sugar, salt, and scallions.
  2. Assemble the wontons. Take each wrapper, and dampen the outer edges with a thin layer of water. Place a teaspoon of the cream cheese filling in the middle, and fold in half into a triangle. Bring the two corners on the long side of the triangle together, and seal with another dab of water. Continue until all the wontons are assembled.
  3. Heat a couple inches of vegetable oil in a small pot until it reaches 375 degrees F. Fry the wontons in batches––about 2 minutes per batch––until golden brown. Definitely use a small pot for this and fry in multiple batches; you’ll use much less oil!
  4. Make the dipping sauce by combining all the sauce ingredients, and serve with the wontons.
